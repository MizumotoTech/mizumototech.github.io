Why start here

Implementation is easier after the risk boundary is visible.

Many teams already know the shape of the problem: Terraform grew unevenly, release gates are informal, runbooks depend on memory, and production changes require too much trust in unwritten steps.

The review pack is intentionally narrow. It documents the current state, separates urgent risks from useful improvements, and creates a follow-up backlog that can be reviewed before anyone changes production systems.

What gets reviewed

The parts of cloud delivery where vague ownership becomes expensive.

01

Infrastructure baseline

Environment boundaries, Terraform state, module structure, IAM patterns, and change flow.

02

Release workflow

Build, validation, deployment path, promotion rules, approval gates, and rollback checkpoints.

03

Operational readiness

Runbooks, escalation triggers, day-2 ownership, known risks, and handover completeness.

04

Cost hygiene signals

Ownership visibility, obvious waste candidates, budget alerting gaps, and safe cleanup sequencing.

Delivery shape

A small engagement with reviewable outputs.

01

Scope

Confirm systems, access level, production sensitivity, decision stakeholders, and exclusions.

02

Map

Review architecture docs, repositories, pipeline definitions, configs, and known pain points.

03

Prioritize

Separate near-term risk reduction from larger follow-up work and record trade-off rationale.

04

Handover

Deliver report, action plan, risk notes, and backlog-ready implementation tasks.

The goal is not to collect generic best practices. The goal is to decide what should be fixed first, by whom, and under which risk boundary.

Fit

Designed for small teams that need senior judgment before more delivery work.

Good fit

  • Small to mid-size product teams on GCP.
  • MSPs, cloud consultancies, and software teams with delivery pressure.
  • Teams that need a practical roadmap before major implementation work.
  • Teams that can provide honest context and review stakeholders.

Not the starting point

  • Full production migration execution.
  • Organization-wide platform redesign.
  • Long-term on-call operation support.
  • Deep SLO or observability program design as the first engagement.
