Why this model

The offer is intentionally narrower than the engineering capability.

The strongest trust signal is restraint. Mizumoto Tech is positioned as a founder-led engineering practice, not a standing delivery bench or generic managed operations provider.

That means the first offer stays concrete: GCP-first review, Terraform foundation, CI/CD bootstrap, runbook and handover support, and practical cost hygiene. Other work can be discussed later when it fits the agreed risk boundary.

Decision model

A simple way to decide how much access the work deserves.

Low-access

Architecture review

Start with documents, repository and pipeline context, known pain points, and stakeholder review.

Controlled

Scoped implementation

Terraform, CI/CD, runbook, or cost hygiene work can follow after scope, access, and stop conditions are agreed.

Sensitive

Direct senior handling

Production-impacting or security-sensitive work requires stricter access boundaries or separate approval.

  • Define what is in scope Systems, environments, repositories, pipelines, access level, and decision stakeholders.
  • Define what is not in scope Major migrations, 24x7 operations, full platform redesign, and deep observability programs are not the default starting point.
  • Define how success is checked Acceptance criteria should describe what another engineer can understand, run, or review from the handover package.

Boundary before delivery

A narrower scope protects both the client and the work.

Serious cloud work starts by reducing ambiguity, not by promising to handle every cloud problem from the first call.

Buyer clarity

The right fit should be clear before discovery starts.

This fits when

  • You need senior review before committing implementation budget.
  • You have GCP, Terraform, CI/CD, or handover risk that needs sequencing.
  • You can provide context and review decisions within agreed timelines.
  • You value conservative access boundaries and clear documentation.

This is not positioned as

  • A 24x7 incident response retainer.
  • A full observability overhaul from the first engagement.
  • A multi-cloud implementation commitment from day one.
  • A guaranteed cost-reduction or compliance-certification service.

Commercial note

Pricing should follow scope, access, and response expectations.

Commercial terms are scoped after discovery. The starting point is the engagement model, access boundary, delivery responsibility, and response expectations.

Lower-sensitivity implementation support may be scoped separately where the delivery boundary and risk profile allow it. High-sensitivity work keeps stricter senior review and access control.
